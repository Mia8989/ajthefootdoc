About

The kid from Houston who became Michigan's Foot Doc.

Alton R. Johnson Jr., DPM, DABPM, FACPM, FASPS, FFPM RCPS (Glasg), CWSP is a podiatric surgeon, wound care specialist, educator, and mentor. This is how he got here, and where he is going.

A career of firsts.

Born in Baton Rouge, Louisiana and raised in Houston, Texas, Dr. Johnson grew up with a commitment to service and to improving the health of underserved communities. That commitment has guided every chapter since, across academic medicine, clinical care, research, and medical education.

His path runs from Barry University in Miami Shores to Kent State University College of Podiatric Medicine, through a chief residency at HCA Florida Aventura Hospital, and into one of the most selective wound care fellowships in the country at Penn Medicine. Along the way he became the first podiatrist to complete the University of Pennsylvania's Post-Doctoral Certificate in Clinical Research, and the only podiatric clinical investigator in the nation selected for the 2020 NIH/National Medical Association Academic Medicine Fellows Program.

Today he is a Clinical Assistant Professor in the Department of Orthopaedic Surgery at the University of Michigan Medical School, where he educates medical students, residents, fellows, and practicing clinicians. His practice centers on limb salvage and the prevention of lower extremity amputations, with expertise across diabetic foot complications, chronic wounds, peripheral arterial disease, venous disease, Charcot neuroarthropathy, reconstructive foot and ankle surgery, and sports-related foot and ankle conditions. He is pursuing a Master of Health Professions Education to sharpen how the next generation learns medicine.

A recognized leader in his field, Dr. Johnson serves as President of the Save A Leg, Save A Life Foundation, a national effort to reduce preventable amputations through education, advocacy, and multidisciplinary collaboration. He has held national leadership roles within the podiatric profession and lectures at regional, national, and international conferences on wound care, limb salvage, diabetic foot management, and emerging healthcare technologies.

Quick answers

Frequently asked.

Who is Dr. Alton Johnson, DPM?

Dr. Alton R. Johnson Jr., DPM is a board-certified podiatric surgeon, Certified Wound Specialist Physician, and Clinical Assistant Professor of Orthopaedic Surgery at the University of Michigan Medical School. Known as AJ The Foot Doc, he specializes in wound care, limb preservation, and diabetic foot management.

What do all those letters after his name mean?

DPM: Doctor of Podiatric Medicine. DABPM: Diplomate of the American Board of Podiatric Medicine. FACPM and FASPS: Fellow of the American College of Podiatric Medicine and of the American Society of Podiatric Surgeons. FFPM RCPS (Glasg): Fellow of the Faculty of Podiatric Medicine, Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow. CWSP: Certified Wound Specialist Physician, American Board of Wound Management.

What is he known for in wound care?

Diabetic foot ulcer management and limb preservation, international education work on the Wound Balance concept with LINK for Wound Healing, and outspoken writing on wound care access and equity, including the amputation crisis affecting African American patients.

What is AJ Educational Empowerment LLC?

His education company, built to empower individuals of all backgrounds through educational services to become tomorrow's leaders. It is the umbrella for his mentorship, speaking, and student programs.

Where did he train?

Barry University (BS), Kent State University College of Podiatric Medicine (DPM), chief residency at HCA Florida Aventura Hospital in Miami, and the Podiatric Clinical Research and Wound Care Fellowship at Penn Medicine. He is currently completing a Master of Health Professions Education at Michigan.
